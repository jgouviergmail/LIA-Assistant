## Our Pledge

As members, contributors, and leaders of this project, we pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Visible or invisible disability
- Ethnicity
- Sex characteristics
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Education
- Socio-economic status
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Caste
- Color
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Encouraged Behaviors

Examples of behaviors that contribute to creating a positive environment:

| Behavior | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Empathy and kindness** | Showing understanding toward other members |
| **Respect** | Accepting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences |
| **Constructive feedback** | Giving and receiving feedback with grace and professionalism |
| **Acknowledging mistakes** | Apologizing, learning from the experience, and improving |
| **Community focus** | Prioritizing what is best for the community as a whole |
| **Inclusive language** | Using welcoming and respectful language |
| **Celebrating contributions** | Recognizing everyone's work, whether large or small contributions |
| **Patience** | Supporting new contributors in their learning journey |
| **Collaboration** | Favoring cooperation over competition |
| **Transparency** | Being clear about decisions and processes |

### Unacceptable Behaviors

Examples of unacceptable behaviors:

| Behavior | Severity |
|----------|----------|
| Harassment of any form, public or private | CRITICAL |
| Sexualized language or imagery, unwelcome advances | CRITICAL |
| Trolling, insulting comments, personal attacks | HIGH |
| Publishing private information without consent (doxxing) | CRITICAL |
| Deliberate intimidation | HIGH |
| Stalking or unwelcome following | CRITICAL |
| Unsolicited photography or recording | HIGH |
| Sustained disruption of discussions | MEDIUM |
| Inappropriate physical contact | CRITICAL |
| Defending or encouraging the above behaviors | HIGH |

## Enforcement Responsibilities

### Project Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They have the right and responsibility to:

- **Remove, edit, or reject** comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- **Temporarily or permanently ban** any contributor for inappropriate behavior
- **Clearly communicate** the reasons for moderation decisions

### Decision Transparency

| Action | Timeline | Process |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Documentation | Immediate | Every decision is documented |
| Communication | < 48h | Affected parties are informed |
| Review | On request | Panel of 2+ maintainers for serious cases |

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Cases of abusive behavior can be reported to enforcement contacts:

| Contact | Usage |
|---------|-------|
| **conduct@lia-assistant.dev** | Incident reporting |
| **conduct-appeal@lia-assistant.dev** | Appealing a decision |
| **conduct-escalation@lia-assistant.dev** | Escalation (cases involving maintainers) |

### Information to Include in a Report

1. Your contact information (for follow-up)
2. Names (real names, usernames, or pseudonyms) of the people involved
3. Description of the incident (what, when, where)
4. Additional context (history, screenshots, logs)
5. Whether the incident is ongoing or likely to recur
6. Whether you have spoken to anyone else about it
7. Any other relevant information

### Confidentiality

All complaints are:

| Guarantee | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Reviewed and investigated | Promptly and fairly |
| Treated confidentially | Always |
| Acknowledged | < 48 hours |
| Fully resolved | < 7 days |

Maintainers are obligated to:

- Maintain the confidentiality of the reporting person
- Not disclose the reporter's identity without consent
- Protect the reporter against retaliation

### Resolution Process

```
1. Acknowledgment (< 48h)
   └── Confirm receipt + assign a responsible person

2. Investigation (< 5 days)
   └── Gather facts, interview, examine evidence

3. Decision (< 7 days total)
   └── Panel of 2+ maintainers → determine violation + response

4. Communication (< 7 days total)
   └── Inform reporter + person concerned

5. Follow-up (30 days after)
   └── Verify behavior change + absence of retaliation
```

### Appeal

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Contact | conduct-appeal@lia-assistant.dev |
| Timeline | 14 days after notification |
| Process | Review by a different panel |
| Final decision | < 14 days after appeal |

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers follow these guidelines to determine the consequences of any violation:

### 1. Correction

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Impact** | Inappropriate language or isolated unprofessional behavior |
| **Consequence** | Private written warning, possible public apology |
| **Duration** | Permanent on record |
| **Examples** | Mildly offensive language, sarcastic comment, unconstructive criticism |

### 2. Warning

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Impact** | Violation through a single incident or series of actions |
| **Consequence** | Warning with interaction restrictions (30-90 days) |
| **Duration** | 30-90 days depending on severity |
| **Examples** | Repeated inappropriate comments, sustained disruption, moderate personal attack |

### 3. Temporary Ban

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Impact** | Serious violation of standards, sustained inappropriate behavior |
| **Consequence** | Ban from all interaction (90 days - 1 year) |
| **Duration** | 90 days to 1 year |
| **Examples** | Harassment, doxxing, repeated inappropriate behavior |

### 4. Permanent Ban

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Impact** | Pattern of violations, severe harassment, aggression toward groups |
| **Consequence** | Permanent ban from all interaction |
| **Duration** | Permanent (review possible after 2 years, exceptional cases) |
| **Examples** | Severe harassment, threats, explicit content, repeated violations |

## LIA-Specific Standards

### Technical Communication

#### In Code Reviews

| Acceptable | Unacceptable |
|------------|--------------|
| "This approach could cause an N+1 performance issue. Have you considered `selectinload()`?" | "This code is terrible, don't you know SQLAlchemy?" |

#### In Issues

| Acceptable | Unacceptable |
|------------|--------------|
| "Thanks for the report! Can you provide the logs so we can debug?" | "This isn't a bug, it's an obvious user error." |

#### In Discussions

| Acceptable | Unacceptable |
|------------|--------------|
| "Interesting idea! An alternative approach would be... What do you think?" | "This idea will never work, that's obvious." |

### Code Contribution

#### Respecting Experience Levels

- Beginner contributors are **encouraged and supported**
- "Basic" questions are **legitimate and welcome**
- Mistakes are **learning opportunities**
- Mentoring is **valued and encouraged**

#### Attribution

- Always **credit** contributions (code, ideas, reviews)
- Never **claim** someone else's work as your own
- Acknowledge **influences and inspirations**

### Linguistic Inclusivity

#### Inclusive Language

- Use **gender-neutral** language
- Avoid **unnecessarily complex** jargon
- Provide **translations** when possible (6 supported languages)
- Be **patient** with non-native speakers

#### Accessibility

- Documentation accessible to **screen readers**
- Code examples with **explanatory comments**
- Diagrams with **text descriptions**

### Responsible and Ethical AI

As an AI assistant project, we have specific responsibilities:

#### LLM Transparency

| Obligation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Document | AI capabilities and limitations clearly explained |
| Honesty | Do not mislead about the origin of responses (human vs AI) |
| Explanations | Decision mechanisms documented (HITL, routing, semantic selection) |
| Costs | Communicate API costs and token consumption |
| Processing | Indicate whether local (E5 embeddings) or cloud (LLM providers) |

#### User Data Protection

| Obligation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Privacy | Comply with GDPR, PII filtering |
| Minimization | Collect only necessary data |
| Security | Fernet OAuth encryption, secured credentials |
| Consent | Never share without authorization |
| Voice/TTS | Do not retain audio data beyond the session |
| Memory Store | Allow users to view/delete long-term memories |

#### Responsible Use of LLMs

| Acceptable | Unacceptable |
|------------|--------------|
| Developing prompts that avoid bias | Creating prompts that circumvent safety guidelines |
| Implementing guardrails against harmful content | Using AI to generate misleading content |
| Testing ethical edge cases | Collecting data for training without consent |
| Using the Skills system for contextual operations | Bypassing HITL confirmations for bulk operations |

#### Skills System (v6.0)

| Obligation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Documentation | Document each skill and its capabilities |
| Limits | Clearly define the limits of each skill |
| Audit | Log skill invocations for auditing |

#### FOR_EACH Bulk Operations (v6.0)

| Obligation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| HITL | Always request confirmation for bulk mutations |
| Limits | Respect `for_each_max` to prevent abuse |
| Preview | Display a preview of actions before execution |

#### Voice HD Mode (v6.0)

| Obligation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Consent | Inform the user of the audio mode being used |
| Costs | Clearly communicate HD vs Standard costs |
| Data | Do not retain generated audio files |

#### Google API Cost Tracking (v6.1)

| Obligation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Transparency | Communicate Google Maps Platform costs to users |
| Audit | Maintain an immutable audit trail of API calls |
| Exports | Allow consumption data export for billing |
| Pricing | Keep Google pricing up to date in the database |

#### Consumption Exports (v6.1)

| Obligation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Confidentiality | Restrict export access to administrators |
| Integrity | Ensure accuracy of exported data |
| Format | Provide standard formats (CSV) for interoperability |

### Handling Sensitive Data

#### Best Practices

| Acceptable | Unacceptable |
|------------|--------------|
| Use `.env.example` with dummy values | Store real emails/contacts in tests |
| Test with synthetic data | Log tokens or secrets in plain text |
| Mask credentials in logs | Share `.env` files with real credentials |
| Use the PII filter in production | Commit secrets in code |

#### In Case of a Potential Leak

1. **Report** immediately to security@lia-assistant.dev
2. **Revoke** compromised credentials
3. **Document** the incident
4. **Notify** affected users if necessary

## FAQ

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if I witness a violation?**

A: Report to conduct@lia-assistant.dev with as many details as possible. You can remain anonymous, but we will not be able to keep you informed of the resolution.

**Q: Can I report an old incident (> 6 months)?**

A: Yes. Our ability to investigate may be limited, but we take all reports seriously.

**Q: What happens if a maintainer violates the Code of Conduct?**

A: Maintainers are held to the same standards. Report to conduct@lia-assistant.dev. For cases involving multiple maintainers, contact conduct-escalation@lia-assistant.dev.

**Q: How can I contest a ban?**

A: Send an email to conduct-appeal@lia-assistant.dev within 14 days. A different panel will review the decision.

**Q: Is criticizing someone else's code allowed?**

A: Yes, **constructive** criticism of code (not the person) is encouraged. Focus on the "what" and "why", not the "who".

**Q: Does the Code apply to private conversations?**

A: Yes, if they concern the project (e.g., DM to discuss a PR). No for personal conversations unrelated to LIA.

**Q: Can I use humor and emojis?**

A: Yes! Light and respectful humor is welcome. Avoid humor that targets groups or individuals.

**Q: How can I help maintain a positive environment?**

A:
- Welcome new contributors
- Give constructive feedback
- Thank contributors
- Report problematic behavior
- Be patient and empathetic
